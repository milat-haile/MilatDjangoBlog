Chemistry

Description

Chemistry is the study of matter, specifically its properties, composition, structure and transformations. Matter is anything with mass that occupies physical space, so chemists may study a variety of solid, liquid or gaseous substances. Chemists typically work to understand what makes up each element or compound and how different elements interact with one another to create reactions. Chemists typically perform research, execute experiments, analyze data and prepare reports and papers detailing their findings. They may apply their studies to fields like engineering, health care, education, manufacturing or law enforcement. Related: Should I Be a Chemist? 11 Reasons To Consider This Role Main branches of chemistry Within this scientific discipline, there are five main branches: Organic chemistry: Organic chemistry studies carbon-containing compounds. Inorganic chemistry: Inorganic chemistry focuses on compounds such as metals and minerals. Physical chemistry: Physical chemistry incorporates physics into chemistry. Biochemistry: Biochemistry studies the chemical processes within living things. Analytical chemistry: Analytical chemistry focuses on the structure and composition of matter.
